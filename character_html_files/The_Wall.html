Wall

From A Wiki of Ice and Fire
(Redirected from The Wall)
Jump to: navigation, search
The Wall by Feliche©
Man of the Night's Watch guarding the Wall by Patrick McEvoy © Fantasy Flight Games

The Wall is a massive barrier of mostly ice stretching across the northern border of the Seven Kingdoms, separating it from the wild lands beyond. The Wall is one hundred leagues long[1][2] (three hundred miles)[3][4] and reaches over seven hundred feet at his highest point,[5] although some foundation blocks in hilly regions, such as near Greyguard, send it nine hundred feet in the air.[6]

Appearing as one of the nine wonders made by man in the book written by Lomas Longstrider,[5][7] the Wall is defended and held by the Sworn Brothers of the Night's Watch, who patrol and guard nineteen castles from the Frostfangs mountain range in the west to the Bay of Seals in the east. Only the Shadow Tower, Castle Black, and Eastwatch-by-the-Sea are garrisoned at the start of A Song of Ice and Fire. Those who join the Watch in order to avoid another punishment can be referred to as sent to the Wall.[8][9][10][11]

Layout

Appearance

See also: Images of the Wall
The Wall by Sabin Boykinov © Fantasy Flight Games

The Wall stretches for three hundred miles[3] and is approximately seven hundred feet tall.[12] It is made of solid ice, stone, and earth,[6] and can be seen for miles round. It appears grey or blue depending on the weather and time of day. The top is wide enough for a dozen mounted knights to ride abreast and it is thicker at the base.[13] The top is wider in man places than the kingsroad, and crushed stone is spread on walkways to aid with footing.[1] According to myth, there are old spells woven into the Wall, to strengthen it and keep creatures of a magical nature such as the Others from passing it.[6][14]

The Wall is straight like a sword from Castle Black to Eastwatch-by-the-Sea, but runs like a snake between Castle Black and the Shadow Tower.[6] Led by Othell Yarwyck, the First Builder, the builders of the Watch monitor and repair the Wall.[15]

Windblown dirt covers the Wall like film, and it often appears pale grey[13] or dull white.[16] The Wall shines blue and crystalline in sunlight,[13][6] glimmers palely in moonlight,[1][12] and glows pink and purple at dawn.[3] The Wall sparkles from melting ice on sunny days, with rivulets of water being compared to weeping.[3][17]

Tunnels have been cut through the Wall at each castle of the Night's Watch. They are sealed with ice and stone wherever castles have been abandoned or in times of danger.[18] Cells chiseled from the ice at the foot of the Wall are used as storerooms[17] or prisons.[19][20]

While Benjen Stark, the First Ranger, believes the Wall is the largest structure built by man,[13] others say the Hightower in Oldtown is even taller,[18] and that the Wall can allegedly be seen from the tower's top.[21]

Castles

The Night's Watch raised nineteen castles to guard the hundred leagues of the Wall, although they never manned more than seventeen at one time.[2] Over the last three hundred years, as the manpower of the Watch has dropped, most of these castles have been abandoned; Old Nan refers to them as "ghost castles".[22]

Only three castles currently remain in use,[1] though sometimes a larger force is sent to one of the abandoned castles for a fortnight or a moon's turn.[23] The manned castles are Castle Black, the largest-maintained castle, located at the northern end of the kingsroad and held by nearly six hundred Brothers,[1] the Shadow Tower, the westernmost of the occupied castles, garrisoned by two hundred men of the Night's Watch,[1] and Eastwatch-by-the-Sea, the port of the Night's Watch with the smallest garrison, located at the eastern end of the Wall.

Defenses

Warming shed on top of the Wall

The builders of the Night's Watch permit the forest to come no closer than half a mile to the Wall.[1][15] However, over time forest has crept back closer to abandoned castles.[1] Rangers patrol the lands beyond the Wall.[15]

The top of the wall is studded with catapults, cranes,[13] and trebuchets,[24] but they are immobile.[24] Additionally, some catapults and trebuchets have been dismantled for repair and never reassembled after being frozen in ice.[1] The Night's Watch can reach the top of the Wall from the southern side via ramps of earth and gravel,[25] winch-drawn cages,[1] and steps of ice, stone, or wood.[25]

The twisting tunnel through the Wall at Castle Black is secured by a thick oaken door and three grates with heavy iron bars, massive chains that must be unlocked, and murder holes,[3][24] while the gate at the Nightfort had an iron portcullis.[25] All tunnels have been sealed except for those at Castle Black, Eastwatch-by-the-Sea, and the Shadow Tower, however.[25]

The galleys of Eastwatch patrol the Bay of Seals in part to catch smugglers who bypass the Wall through the sea and trade weapons to wildlings.[26] In the west the southern Frostfangs and the Gorge make the terrain impassable for all but small groups of raiders.[27]

Tunnel through the Wall, barred by heavy iron bars

The Wall provides a tremendous tactical advantage to its defenders, enabling a small group to hold off a much larger force. Defenders are generally beyond the range of bow shot or catapult and in turn rely on archery, catapult, and dropped objects to deter an assault. In particular, barrels containing rocks frozen in ice are effective in dispersing a mass charge.[24]

Attackers must rely on one of two tactics: a direct assault on one of the few well-defended gates, or scaling the Wall in small groups and attacking a gate from the rear. The gates have been designed with features such as murder holes and limited access that make them difficult to force.[24] In an extreme case, gates can be filled with rock and ice, thus becoming as impervious as any other part of the wall.[28]

Scaling the Wall involves similar difficulties. Wildlings climb the northern side of the Wall with hempen rope, stone hammers, ice axes, and spiked boots.[6] A team of determined climbers can reach the top, but only if undetected by defenders, who can easily knock them off with dropped objects or archery. The Wall is thought to "defend itself", disgorging sheets of its own ice.[6]

History

The building of the Wall (by Chase Stone©)

The Wall was made to defend the realms of the First Men,[29][30] and the men of the Night's Watch have reportedly defended the Wall against Others and other monsters[5] for eight thousand years[31] since the end of the Long Night.[5]

According to legend, the Wall was built by Brandon the Builder[32] with the assistance of children of the forest and giants,[5] and it is protected with ancient spells and sorcery.[25] Supposedly, Brandon placed foundations along heights wherever possible.[6] Also according to legend, the Wall is vulnerable to the Horn of Joramun.[6]

Maesters dispute if the base of the Wall was made of stone, but they generally believe builders of the Watch used sledges to transport ice cut from frozen lakes in the haunted forest. However, the severely-reduced Night's Watch at the present time is limited to maintenance duty.[15][5]

Some Kings-Beyond-the-Wall have led hosts of free folk south, but all have been broken at the Wall by the Night's Watch or, if they managed to evade the Watch and pass south, by House Stark of Winterfell.[33]

When King Jaehaerys I Targaryen visited Winterfell, Queen Alysanne Targaryen flew on Silverwing to visit the Wall.[18] Her dragon's refusal to cross the Wall puzzles and disturbs the queen.[34]

Recent Events

The Wall and the tunnel at Castle Black in Game of Thrones

A Game of Thrones

When Jon Snow joins the Night's Watch, Tyrion Lannister accompanies him north to see the Wall.[35] Tyrion spends several weeks riding the Wall and conversing with the officers.[13] After taking the winch cage to the top, Tyrion walks the Wall with Jon, who has drawn night guard, and the youth's direwolf, Ghost.[1] Samwell Tarly is afraid of taking the cage to the top.[12] After having a nightmare of the crypt of Winterfell, Jon climbs the Wall and walks until sunrise.[17]

A Clash of Kings

Heading beyond the Wall, Jon and Sam participate in the great ranging led by Lord Commander Jeor Mormont.[36][35]

A Storm of Swords

Mance Rayder, the King-Beyond-the-Wall, has free folk search the Frostfangs[37] and the Milkwater[38] for the Horn of Winter, which allegedly can bring down the Wall.[6][38]

Feigning allegiance to the wildling cause, Jon climbs the Wall with a group of wildlings. Sheets of ice break off, leading to the death of Jarl.[6] They descend from the Wall at Greyguard.[18]

After the mutiny at Craster's Keep, Samwell and Gilly flee south[39] and pass through the Wall via the Black Gate below the Nightfort. Sam informs Bran Stark and his companions how to pass north of the Wall.[25]

Mance sends his chiefs and captains to attack various places along the Wall, spreading the Night's Watch thin.[18] Styr leads the attack on Castle Black, the defense of which is led by Jon and Donal Noye.[40] Mance's wildling host then attacks the Wall from the north with giants and mammoths, and Jon takes command of the defense after Donal's death.[24] During a parley, Mance displays a giant horn to Jon but is reluctant to use it, as he hopes to lead the free folk through the Wall and seek refuge from Others.[41] The wildlings are then defeated by King Stannis Baratheon and the Watch in the battle beneath the Wall.[41]

Following the battle, Jon and Melisandre ascend the Wall to meet Stannis, who offers to name Jon as Lord of Winterfell.[42] Jon declines, however, and ends up being chosen as the 998th Lord Commander of the Night's Watch.[43]

A Dance with Dragons

Free folk who submit to Stannis are allowed to pass through the Wall.[44] Bowen Marsh objects, preferring instead to seal the gates.[44] Some of the free folk accept Jon's offer to help man the Wall.[45] Jon sends Val to find Tormund Giantsbane in the haunted forest,[46] and Tormund eventually agrees to pass through the Wall.[47]

Lord Commander Snow puts plans in motion to man most of the castles. These include:

  • Westwatch-by-the-Bridge, garrisoned by a small company from the Night's Watch.[48]
  • Sentinel Stand, garrisoned by a small company from the Watch.[49]
  • Greyguard, garrisoned by an unnamed steward from the Shadow Tower.[44]
  • Stonedoor, given to Soren Shieldbreaker to settle and garrison.[16] Borroq is to join him.[16]
  • Hoarfrost Hill, still empty as Jon has not yet named a commander.[16]
  • Icemark, garrisoned with thirty men under the command of Bedwyck, twenty from the Night's Watch and ten of Stannis's men.[50]
  • Nightfort, given to Stannis as his seat, but it will take time for the builders to make it suitable for habitation.[51]
  • Deep Lake, garrisoned with free folk, possibly by Halleck.[52]
  • Queensgate, given to Morna White Mask to settle and garrison.[16]
  • Oakenshield, given to Tormund to settle and garrison.[16]
  • Woodswatch-by-the-Pool, garrisoned by a small company from the Watch.[53]
  • Sable Hall, garrisoned with free folk, possibly by Halleck.[52]
  • Rimegate, still empty as Jon has not yet named a commander.[16]
  • Long Barrow, garrisoned with spearwives under the command of Iron Emmett[46][47] and Eddison Tollett.[54]
  • The Torches, garrisoned by a small company from the Watch.[55]
  • Greenguard, given to Devyn Sealskinner to settle and garrison.[16]

Builders begin construction on a new switchback stair to replace the ruined one at Castle Black.[51] Jon intends for a series of beacon towers to be built atop the Wall.[50] Jon decides to hang Janos Slynt from the Wall for insubordination, but he changes his mind and instead beheads Janos with Longclaw.[50]

Melisandre thinks she is stronger than she has ever been while at the Wall.[56] She burns what is claimed to be the Horn of Winter,[44] but Tormund later explains to Jon that Mance never found the horn which could bring down the Wall.[47] Jon has a nightmare of defending the top of the Wall from the dead, including wildlings, Night's Watch, and his half brother, Robb Stark.[47] The Lord Commander is eventually betrayed in the mutiny at Castle Black.[16]
